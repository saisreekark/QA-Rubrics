Deal Split	
This guidelines and requirements for implementing deal splits to recognize and incentivize multiple contributors involved in closing a deal.

Here's a summary of the key points:
Purpose: To ensure equitable distribution of attainment based on individual impact, with greater shares allocated to those who significantly influenced the deal's success.
Important Notes:
All split allocations and approvals must be finalized and documented before a deal is closed.
Total split allocation must equal 100%.
Minimum individual split is 10%.
Applicable to Bookings and Subscriptions, but not Revenue or CoW.
Before You Begin (Guidelines):
Obtain all required approvals via email before the deal's close date.
Ensure distribution is clearly documented and adds up to 100%.
Ensure the deal is officially closed.
FSR must wait for the deal to reflect in Rainmaker, then create a Vector case requesting a deal split, and submit a deal split request form.
Requirements:
FSRs need to confirm their role and product eligibility in the T&Cs.
Additional restrictions may apply for splits involving Specialists.
Only agreements made within the current fiscal year are valid.
Submissions must be made within the quarter of the deal booking date and within published quarterly deadlines.
Distribution agreements and approvals must be documented and timestamped in writing before the deal is closed in Vector.
Submissions must include impacted Sales Googlers and their documented approval (e.g., FSR/KAE/D, FSM, Sales Director, RSO; VP approval for TBH NALs), and any justification.
Generic Timelines:
Deal reflects in Rainmaker approximately 3 business days after booking.
Split can take up to 72 hours to be fully processed and reflect in Rainmaker from case creation.
FAQs:
For account split inquiries, contact your RSO.
Approvals dated after the deal close date will be denied, subject to Compensation Exception Committee (CEC) review.

SPIFF
This document explains what a sales SPIFF (Sales Performance Incentive Fund) is, providing guidance on eligibility, common issues, and how to get assistance.

The SOP in the "Compensation/Attainment Help | SPIFF" document is a guide for the Compensation/Attainment (C/A) team on managing the Sales Performance Incentive Fund (SPIFF) process, covering eligibility, payout, and case management.Key SOP Procedures
What is SPIFF? A SPIFF, or sales performance incentive fund, is a commission paid to sales representatives for meeting or exceeding sales goals. Rules for eligibility and winning criteria are specific to each SPIFF.
Payout: SPIFFs are paid quarterly along with the sales bonus amount. The amounts are converted to local currency using a specific conversion rate (
go/bonusscale-2025
).
Viewing Payout: Sellers view their SPIFF payment details under Quarter Adjustments in the Overview and Bonus Statement tabs in Rainmaker (
go/rainmaker
). SPIFFs are not a separate line item on the paycheck; they are summed with the quarterly Sales Bonus.
Case Management (Triaging Steps)

When a seller creates a Vector case under the SPIFF category, the agent's triaging steps are as follows:
Determine Query Type:
If the query is on payment (PII): The Queue Manager must assign the case directly to Kyle (Comp Admin SPOC).
If the query is on payment date and sales rep eligibility: The analyst can work and respond to the query.
Check Eligibility and Payout Status:
Eligibility: The agent must check if the seller is eligible based on the criteria for the specific SPIFF type. If eligible, they will be paid in the next quarter.
Dashboard Check: The agent should check the SPIFF Dashboard (
go/salescomp-spiffs
) to see if the SPIFF has already been paid.
Seller Guidance (If Already Paid):
If the SPIFF is already paid, the agent guides the seller: "Your pay slip will not show a separate line for the SPIFF, it will be summed up with your Q1 sales bonus so please refer to Rainmaker (go/rainmaker) for the expected payment amount."
Investigation (If Not Paid):
If no data is found in the dashboard, the agent continues triaging to determine why the seller didn't receive it (e.g., they may not be eligible). The agent needs to confirm with the seller which SPIFF they are inquiring about to start the investigation.
Case Follow-up and Closure:
Agents keep the case open for up to 5 working days with 3 follow-up attempts. If there is no response, the case is closed on the 5th working day with a canned response.
Escalation: If a seller disagrees with the rejection of the SPIFF based on eligibility criteria, the agent needs to escalate the case to Kyle for further review.

Purpose: To help C/A employees understand and resolve SMB compensation/attainment issues.
Scope: Applies to the GTM Cloud Helpdesk, focusing on 9001 and 8001 plans for SMB Workspace sales.
Key Checks: Verify the account segment is SMB in Vector, and the seller's manager is under Sharon Prosser. Ensure the correct issue category/sub-category, a clear case description, and the Opportunity ID are provided.

Understanding the Cloud Hierarchy and Its Impact on Sales Compensation at Google Cloud
"Understanding the Cloud Hierarchy and Its Impact on Sales Compensation at Google Cloud," explains how Google Cloud structures its products and services and how this impacts sales compensation.

Part 1 Metadata (Trix) :(Finance product Hierarchy dashboard) Cloud Product Hierarchy Explorer (go/cloudhierarchy ) 
The Cloud Hierarchy Dataset is an eligibility matrix that details which revenue activities for Google Cloud Platform (GCP), Google Workspace, Maps, and Chrome are eligible for compensation.
The data is organized into a four-level hierarchy:
Level 1: Top-level business areas (e.g., GCP, Google Workspace).
Level 2: Product families within Level 1 (e.g., GCP Security, GCP Analytics).
Level 3: Specific product areas within Level 2 (e.g., Cloud Bigtable, Cloud Spanner).
Level 4: Individual products or SKUs.
For most of the listed products, "Total Revenue" and "Incremental Revenue" are marked as Eligible for compensation under specific plan IDs. Conversely, many specific booking types, such as "Apigee Bookings," are consistently listed as Not Eligible. The document provides a clear, structured view that allows a compensation chatbot to quickly determine compensation eligibility for various transaction types

Part 2 Metadata (Doc): Core Components of Sales Compensation Systems within a Cloud Hierarchy
This document explains how Google Cloud links products (SKUs) to sales compensation. It covers:
SKUs: Unique product identifiers for tracking sales.
FPH: A hierarchical system for product categorization, used for reporting and comp plans.
Compensation Plans: Rules defining how sales performance translates to earnings, using FPH for eligibility.
Cloud Hierarchy: A dashboard (go/cloudhierarchy) visualizing SKU-FPH-Comp Plan relationships.
Key Interaction: Sales (SKUs) are mapped to FPH, and Comp Plans use FPH to define payout rules (including baselines, watermarks, multipliers).
Product Groups: Groupings of products used for reporting, forecasting, and setting quotas.
Key Terms: (SKU, FPH, Quota, Attainment, OTV, Baseline, Watermark, Multiplier, Accelerator, Plan ID, NAL, Cloud Hierarchy).
BPH: Business Product Hierarchy, FPH often defaults to BPH, but Product Finance Vertical teams may make adjustments.
Use Cases: System design, architecture explanations, integration guides, and data architecture.

Billing ID Moves
The SOP is intended to help Cloud GTM HelpDesk Support Specialists, Training Teams, Quality Analysts, and Managers triage cases under the Billing ID Moves Issue Category. It supports mapping four types of BIDs to a customer account: Argentum (AR), Vanilla (VA), Dasher (DA), and SAP.Procedure

The process for a Billing ID Move involves several steps, depending on the scenario:
Request Initiation: A Seller can raise a request via:
Destiny (BACS): An automated Billing Account Claim System, for most cases.
Helpdesk (HD) Case: Created for Roomba Fallout scenarios (e.g., SAP BID, Unmapping, Graveyard accounts, Account Splits, Google Cloud owned, bulk requests), with an agent assigned to work manually.
Triage and Validation: The Cloud GTM Helpdesk Agent:
Triage cases based on the issue category and follows a detailed set of BID Validation Steps (Steps 1-18 in the SOP).
Gathers or requests necessary information (BID, Desired Account ID, Opportunity ID, etc.).
Checks for bulk requests (>5 BIDs require an internal consult and a specific bulk template).
Validates account types (e.g., checking if the Desired Account is a Partner type, which requires routing to the PSS Team).
Resolution:
The agent asks for necessary acknowledgements from respective account owners and RSOs (Regional Sales Strategy and Operations).
Once the solution is provided, the agent informs the seller and closes the case as "Issue Resolved."
In Roomba fallout scenarios, DataOps processes the mapping post-Helpdesk approvals and form-based execution.
Timeline
New Roomba request: Completed within 24 hours.
Vector Acknowledgement, Notification, and Submission of forms (for manual cases): Completed within 10 calendar days.
Escalation: If the Seller is not satisfied or the request is rejected, they can Escalate the case via Escalate Functionality on Vector.
Routing Teams

Cases are transferred to different teams based on the issue description:
| Issue Description | Transfer to |
| Support Type | CS-PSS |
| Partner Type | PSS Team |
| Order related Issues | OMPF |
| Merge BID’s, Create BID, Create Payment profile, BMOD issues, Online Billing Issues, Billing Legal Name and Address Change, Invoice Disputes, Migrate existing Billing Accounts | Cloud Billing Support |
Decision Points
When to raise a Roomba request (automated via Destiny): In case of BID types VA (Vanilla), DA (Dasher), and AR (Argentum).
When to take a case via vector acknowledgment and submit a form (manual HD case): In case of BID type SAP, Split, and Fallout Scenarios.
Definitions
BIM (Billing ID Move): A process to ensure a payment account and its spend are flowing to the correct end-customer account for accurate attainment.
PAM (Payment Account Move): Former name for BIM.
BACS (Billing Account Claim System): A Destiny-based automated system for resolving most Billing ID mapping issues and the initial intake system.
RSO (Regional Sales Strategy and Operations): Supports Field Sales Representatives (FSRs) and works on Sales Strategy.
CEC (Escalation process for users to receive one-off approval via a board within COE): Used for exceptions.
AR (Argentum): Primary identifier for the Argentum monetiser (GCP).
DA (Dasher): Primary identifier for the Dasher Commerce monetiser (Google Workspace).
SAP (Systems Applications and Products in Data Processing): A primary identifier; SAP BID movements are currently not supported by Roomba and require a manual Helpdesk case.

Incremental revenue:The document, "2025 GCP Incrementality and GWS Subscription Watermark | Support Guide," provides a detailed overview of the concepts, calculations, and validation procedures for Watermarks and Baselines in the context of the FY25 sales compensation plan, specifically for Google Cloud Platform (GCP) and Google Workspace (GWS).

Here is a summary of the core concepts:1. Watermark vs. Baseline
Watermark (for Bookings/Subscriptions): A reference point, typically a prior level of spend or commitment, used to determine the Incremental Annual Contract Value (iACV) for new deals (like EAs, GCP Commits, and qualifying subscriptions).
Formula: iACV = Net ACV - Watermark. Only the value above the watermark is eligible for bookings attainment.
Baseline (for Incremental Revenue): An annual account hurdle based on prior Q4 spend that must be met before a seller receives Incremental Revenue attainment.
General Formula: Baseline = (Q4 Parent Account Spend) * 4 * 70%. Only revenue above this amount counts toward the Incremental Revenue target.
2. Watermark Calculation (for EAs and GCP Commits)

The watermark is the MAX of two components, calculated at the Parent Account Level (with some exceptions):
Component 1: Existing Spend (Prior Quarter ARR): Based on the Annualized Run Rate (ARR) from the last full quarter prior to the booking date. It is calculated as (Prior Quarter Sales Revenue * 4) and includes products like GCP Core, Looker, Apigee, etc.
Channel Specificity: A key FY25 change dictates that some products (e.g., GCP Core) are Cross-Channel (prior spend from direct and reseller counts), while others (e.g., Workspace, Cloud-Security) are Channel-Specific (only prior spend from the same channel as the new deal counts).
Marketplace Cap: Marketplace ARR contribution is capped at 25% of the total calculated watermark value.
Component 2: Prior Opportunity ACV: The ACV of the most recent relevant prior EA/Commit opportunity tagged in the system.
3. Workspace (GWS) Watermark Calculation

This is an aggregated annual value for a child account, predicting the expected renewal value.
Calculation: Sum of:
ACV of expiring Commit contracts within the plan term.
(Aggregate Flex bookings in Dec of prior year) * 12.
Certain Term-Reset ACV adjustments.
Incoming GWS bookings are compared against this aggregate watermark cumulatively (Residual Watermark concept).
4. What is Not Eligible for Watermark
AI Subscription Products (GenAI) and specific AI Bookings.
Penalties and fees not related to product consumption.
Standalone GCP Subscriptions (with exceptions).

1. Finding Marketplace Revenue (Documents [6] & [9])

This guide is for Sales Comp Analysts to triage Marketplace Revenue cases from sellers, emphasizing that Marketplace revenue is complicated, so you shouldn't assume anything.

Key Resources & Tools:
Seller-Facing Dashboard: go/findisv (for sellers to identify attainable solutions).
Installments Lookup: SalesComp Marketplace Installments Lookup and P2P Marketplace Prepaid Installments Revenue for prepaid installment details.
Eligibility Lookup: Marketplace SKU eligibility Lookup to check SKU eligibility and solution names/IDs.
Data Table: concord.service_doppler.cloud_revenue_sales_comp_dremel (SalesComp view of Doppler with marketplace-specific installment columns).
Eligibility Check: google.GCP_MP_comp_eligible_listings (If a listing is not in this table, it is not eligible).
Basic Triage Strategy: Immediately go upstream into Doppler, as this bypasses eligibility rules and 100% discounting edge cases.
Top 5 Marketplace Revenue Misconceptions:
Misconception: Always getting the full ACV of deals closed in 2025 for security partners (Palo Alto, Exabeam, Splunk, Wiz, Oracle).
Reality: ACV only applies to installments with a duration greater than one year. Installments landing in future years are not attainable in the current year, and attainment only occurs when installments actually land (are invoiced).
Misconception: Still getting prorated revenue for security partner deals closed in prior years.
Reality: For these partners, sellers are rewarded for installment invoices that land in the current year (2025). Prior year deals with installments landing in 2025 are attainable.
Misconception: Monthly prorated revenue is calculated by dividing the deal TCV by the months of the term.
Reality: Deal TCV is never factored into proration. Proration is determined by a given installment's amount and duration (start and end dates) and is calculated at a daily rate.
Misconception: Only a handful of ISV Partners can have discounts/credits.
Reality: This is FALSE. More than 75% of marketplace SKUs in 2023 had some form of discount applied, and sellers are paid on net to Google.
Misconception: Postpay marketplace deals will generate predictable monthly revenue.
Reality: This is FALSE. Postpay is like GCP consumption against a commit, meaning revenue flows based on usage, and there can be months with no revenue.

Misconception: Apigee revenue is consumption revenue.
Reality: It is NOT consumption revenue (except for PAYGO SKUs). Revenue is dictated by Google revenue recognition rules, requiring a signed contract with order information entered into the system and the product to be provisioned.

Debook/ Rebook

The Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is designed to investigate and resolve discrepancies in seller attainment and payment calculations caused by debook and rebook transactions or underlying system issues.
Purpose: To identify the root cause of attainment and payment discrepancies stemming from debook/rebook transactions and to determine the correct corrective action in relevant systems.
Scope: The procedure applies to analyzing booking data (specifically debook and rebook transactions) across systems like Enhanced Agreement Main (EAM), Renewal Data Mart (RDM), and Incremental Bookings Layer (IBL). The analysis covers their impact on Annual Contract Value (ACV), Total Contract Value (TCV), Watermarks, and ultimately, seller attainment.
Key Issues Addressed: The SOP focuses on issues like incorrect booking dates, inconsistent TCV/ACV, timing mismatches between debooks and rebooks across fiscal periods, missing cancellation records, and account mapping errors.
Triage and Resolution Steps

The analysis and corrective process is broken down into a multi-step procedure to diagnose the root cause, followed by a set of resolution steps based on the findings.I. Analysis Procedure (Triage Steps)
Gather Initial Information: Collect details on the discrepancy, including the Account ID, Order ID, product, relevant dates/transactions, and the observed issue (e.g., attainment overstated/understated).
Extract Data: Pull data for the relevant account/order and time period from key systems:
Renewal Data Mart (RDM): For watermark data.
Enhanced Agreement Main (EAM): For all relevant booking data.
Incremental Bookings Layer (IBL): For data consumed by the payment system.
Perform Initial Data Alignment & Comparison:
Compare total booking amounts (TCV, ACV, NRE) across EAM and IBL to check for data flow issues.
Compare EAM totals against RDM watermark totals for the relevant fiscal year(s).
Analyze Transactions (Debooks/Rebooks):
Filter EAM data to identify negative (debook) and positive (rebook) transactions.
Check if debook and rebook dates and amounts align (ideally same date, offsetting amounts).
Look for patterns like large debooks or timing differences across reporting periods.
Drill Down into Order Details:
Examine detailed lines in EAM using Order ID or Agreement ID.
Check Start Date, End Date, and the Cancellation Reason for debooked orders (may require looking up in SAP if fully canceled).
Investigate ACV Calculation: Review if ACV is prorated correctly over the remaining service term versus the original total contract term, as incorrect proration leads to misstatements.
Analyze Watermark Impact: Check which orders contribute to the account's watermark and investigate if early renewals are causing misalignment between the booking date/attainment period and the expected watermark timing.
II. Resolution Steps

Based on the identified root cause, the corrective action is prioritized as follows:
Prioritize Source System Correction (Order Management/SAP):
Required for TCV errors or fundamental data errors on active orders, as this ensures correct invoicing.
Also for wrong booking dates on new orders.
Correct in EAM (Booking Date Overrides):
Used if source correction is not feasible (e.g., historical booking date issues, or if source changes won't reflect downstream correctly/timely).
Requires proper documentation, rationale, and approvals (Finance/Policy).
Manual Overrides in Payment Layer:
Required for issues impacting closed fiscal periods (e.g., prior year attainment) since system changes in EAM will not retroactively fix past attainment.
Requires documenting the adjustment amount and obtaining necessary approvals.
Address System Gaps/Policy Alignment: Communicate cases that highlight system limitations (like ACV proration logic or early renewal watermark handling) to relevant system/policy owners for future enhancements.
Communication: Inform all relevant stakeholders (sales, sales operations, finance, system teams) of the findings and the chosen resolution path.

Quota Issues

This document serves as a guide for sellers experiencing discrepancies or having questions about their sales quotas. It establishes that the Rainmaker system is the official source of truth for quotas, which represent sales targets and are crucial for compensation.

Key Points:
Quota Adjustments: Cloud leadership may adjust quotas mid-year, often due to shifting priorities like a new focus on AI.
Helpdesk Role: The Cloud GTM Helpdesk team can analyze account activity to help understand quota changes but cannot directly assign, modify, or override quotas. All adjustments require approval from the Comp Exception Committee (CEC).
Common Quota-Affecting Factors: These include data quality or non-data quality moves (like account reassignments or mergers), account and territory changes, new product launches, account graduation/segmentation, proration/seasonality, and approved exceptions.
Possible Outcomes After Analysis:
Quota is accurate: Confirmed correct, often due to data/non-data quality moves.
Quota is inaccurate but fixable: A discrepancy is found, and a path to resolution (potentially raising an exception) is recommended.
System Bug: A rare instance where a system bug affects the quota, requiring collaboration with other teams (Eng, Comp Admin, etc.).
Generic Timelines:
Analysis for issues stemming from data/non-data quality moves is expected within 72 hours.
Resolution for a system bug can range from days up to weeks.
Downward quota adjustments via a CEC case may take up to several weeks to review and adjust if approved.

Submit a case to the CEC.

=== DOCUMENT: Plan_Summary ===
The provided documents consist of the 2025 Cloud General Incentive Compensation Terms & Conditions (Ts & Cs), the 2025 Cloud SMB Sales Policy, and the Vector Account Governance and SPIFF Playbook.

Here is a summary of the key policies and topics covered:1. 2025 Cloud General Incentive Compensation Terms & Conditions (Ts & Cs)

This document serves as the overarching guide for incentive compensation for Cloud Sellers in 2025.

Key Sections:
Policy Statements: Covers essential topics like At-Will Employment, Confidentiality, Seller Eligibility, Ethics and Compliance, and the Cloud Audit and Assurance Policy.
Plan Details: Outlines the Sales Compensation structure, including Sales Bonus, Sales Acceleration Bonus, Draws, Special Performance Incentive Funds (SPIFFs), OTV Cap, Chargebacks, Sales Bonus Payment and Timing, and the Windfall Policy.
Seller Events: Details how sales compensation is handled for events like new hires (Nooglers), Role Changes, Promotions, Leaves, Rotations, Transfers, and Termination of Employment.
Territory, Account and Quota Assignment: Defines policies for Territory Assignment, Account Assignment, Quota Assignment, and specifies the Source of Truth for these assignments.
Attainment Components: Provides detailed rules for calculating attainment based on Revenue (including PSSA, Incremental Revenue, and iACV for Subscription), Bookings (including iACV for EAs and GCP Commits), and other components like Mandiant Consulting, PSO and Learning Services, Back-to-Back (B2B), SAP, Workspace Bookings/Watermark/Term Reset, Large Bookings Bonus, and Management By Objectives (MBO).
In-Year Policies: Describes the processes for In-Year Changes, including those related to Data Quality (e.g., Account Merge, Hierarchy Changes) and Non-Data Quality (e.g., M&As, Account Type Changes, Account Movement for various FSRs and segments), and In-Year Splits. It also covers policies for handling Sales Bonus Disputes.
2. 2025 Cloud SMB Sales Policy (Supplemental to Ts & Cs)

This policy specifically outlines rules for the Small and Medium Business (SMB) sales teams for Workspace, GCP New Biz, BI Sales & CE, and Security Sales & CE.

Key Rules:
Segmentation: The SMB sales team focuses on accounts not included in Field segments, categorized into six geographic regions and further divided by sub-regions/countries and seat segments.
SMB Workspace NB (New Business): Sellers own new Workspace accounts ("Net New Accounts") and accounts they closed from the last two performance cycles ("Following Two Quarter Accounts"). Territories are based on location, seats, and opportunity source.
Attainment: Granted once a GWS Core SKU is paid. Individual contributor FTEs do not receive attainment for accounts with less than 50 paid seats, with exceptions for the "10x Proof of Concept."
SMB Workspace AM (Account Management): Sellers own existing Workspace SMB accounts ("AM Core Accounts") and focus on upselling to SKUs with a higher list price.
Attainment: Calculated based on "Incremental ARR" (Annual Recurring Revenue) at the domain level. User Increase opportunities must meet a threshold of >= 10% of the account's seats with a 10 seat minimum for incremental seats.
Territory Movement: If an SMB account moves outside a rep's seat segment by +/- 50 seats, it should be passed to the appropriate rep. Specific exceptions are detailed for Proof of Concept and Rapid Growth Accounts.
Salesforce Requirements: Strict requirements are set for SFDC opportunities (activities, creation date, uniqueness, and "Closed Won" stage) to be eligible for attainment.
Attainment Amount: Credit is based on Incremental ARR. A 1.25x multiplier applies for AM upgrades (moving to a higher list price core GWS SKU or Business to Enterprise SKU family) for non-PDLs.
3. Vector Account Governance: Account Changes

This document details the updated rules for changing account names and addresses in the Vector system to improve data accuracy.

Key Change Categories:
Allowed: Changes matching the legal name/address, alignment with first-party (1P) and third-party (3P) resources without creating duplicates, and cosmetic changes (spelling, adding "LLC," local language names).
Restricted (Manual Review Required): Changes when a duplicate account already exists (merging via Destiny is recommended), "Doing Business As" (DBA) name changes, and all other changes.
Legal Name/Address Changes: Manual review is always required, and they are restricted if the current field account owner rejects the request, or if the seller fails to provide valid proof (D&B, Crunchbase, etc.), or if a duplicate account is found.
4. SPIFF Playbook

This playbook defines the use, governance, and process for Special Performance Incentive Funds (SPIFFs).

Key Principles:
Definition: SPIFFs are special incentives to drive incremental performance above the existing compensation plan, based on objective metrics.
Use Cases: Used for driving specific strategy adjustments, increasing focus on an element not in the core plan, and global strategic initiatives.
Not for: Driving performance on a metric already compensated, increasing focus on a product subset, using excess budget, spot bonuses, or replacing strong management.
Governance:
Must be Objective, Necessary, Executable, and Supported & Funded (VP+ support and earmarked budget).
Limit of 3 concurrent SPIFFs for FSRs/CEs (2 in Q1) and 1 concurrent SPIFF for other roles (SMB, Partner Roles, Specialists).
Process: Requires a formal proposal submission, review (at the latest 3 weeks prior to the start of the quarter), finalization of details, and approval. Payout must align with the quarterly cycle (approximately 60 days following quarter end).
Non-Compensatory Levers: Other methods for driving behavior, like gamifying without monetary reward, OKR metrics, and public recognition, are encouraged. Sales competitions without monetary or in-kind rewards are limited to 2 contests per role, per quarter.
